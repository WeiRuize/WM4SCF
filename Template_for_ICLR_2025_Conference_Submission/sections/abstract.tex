We propose \textbf{Security~Cuff}, a dual-layer inference-time defense
for deployed Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models that detects and
intercepts backdoor-triggered actions before they cause irreversible
physical harm.
As VLA weights are increasingly updated through cloud-edge continuous
learning pipelines, training-time defenses lose their
centralized-access assumption, while existing inference-time monitors
still focus on accidental failures or visual trigger reconstruction
rather than post-encoding hidden-state and trajectory-level signals.
Our key observation is a \emph{physical consequence invariant}: if a
backdoor is designed to induce dangerous behavior, the compromised
rollout must ultimately surface a harmful trajectory, regardless of
trigger form.
Security Cuff exploits this with a fast hidden-state screen and a slow
consequence-aware verifier based on world-model rollout.
On \texttt{[benchmark]} against \texttt{[N]} attacks,
Security~Cuff reduces the catastrophic failure rate to
\texttt{[X\%]} while maintaining task success within
\texttt{[Y\%]} of the clean baseline at \texttt{[Z]\,ms}
overhead per step.
